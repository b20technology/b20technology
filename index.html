FAQ

Common questions.

Is B20 affiliated with Robinhood?

No. B20 Technology builds on the public, permissionless Robinhood Chain network, but is an independent company with no formal partnership, endorsement, or sponsorship from Robinhood Markets, Inc.

Have B20's smart contracts been audited?

Not yet by a third party. Contracts are open source, MIT-licensed, and intentionally minimal — a third-party audit is planned for Q4 2026, listed on the roadmap.

Does B20 ever hold my funds or private keys?

No. Every action — deploying a token, locking liquidity — is signed directly from your own wallet. B20's infrastructure is non-custodial by design.

What does the $0.10 fee cover?

A flat network fee, calculated live in ETH at current prices, charged once per token launch to support platform operations. It's shown to you before you confirm the transaction.

Testnet or mainnet — which should I use first?

Testnet. We recommend testing your full token configuration on Robinhood Chain Testnet before deploying with real funds on mainnet.
